Panipuri

Description

Pani Puri is a beloved Indian street food snack consisting of crispy puri shells filled with a tangy and spicy flavored water, along with a mixture of mashed potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and chutneys. It offers a burst of flavors and a fun, interactive eating experience.

Ingredients

  • Puri shells: Crispy, hollow spheres made from semolina or wheat flour.
  • Pani (flavored water): A tangy and spicy water mixture made with tamarind, mint, coriander, chaat masala, black salt, and spices.
  • Potatoes: Boiled and mashed potatoes seasoned with spices like chaat masala.
  • Chickpeas: Boiled chickpeas used as a filling along with the potatoes.
  • Onions: Finely chopped onions that add a crunch and sharpness to the filling.
  • Chutneys: Sweet and tangy chutneys, typically made with tamarind and dates, and a spicy green chutney made with mint and coriander.
  • Sev: Thin, crispy strands made from gram flour (besan) that are sprinkled on top for added texture.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the flavored water (pani) by blending mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chili, tamarind pulp, chaat masala, black salt, and water. Strain and adjust the seasoning.

  2. Boil and mash potatoes. Season with chaat masala and black salt. Boil chickpeas separately.

  3. Make a small hole in each puri shell and fill it with the potato mixture and chickpeas.

  4. Arrange the stuffed puris on a serving plate.

  5. Pour the flavored water into a jug or bowl.

  6. Just before serving, pour a spoonful of flavored water into each puri.

  7. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors by eating each Pani Puri in one bite!
